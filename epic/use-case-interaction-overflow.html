Use Case/Interaction Flow

Marta Gets 'er Done!

Marta Sees an Issue

Marta is sitting at home, watching Golden Girls, when an email alert appears on her phone. It notifies her that there has been an approved revision to the Wikipedia page for her dear great-uncle Al. Always vigilant, Marta waits until the next commercial break and follows a link in the email to Wikipedia. She sees that the new revision has added carving wooden ducks to his list of hobbies. Knowing the hatred Al had for ducks, she is immediately suspicious. She calls her grandfather and confirms that Al not only despised ducks, he only modeled with clay and never carved wood. Feeling vindicated, Marta returns to the Wikipedia article and submits a revision removing the misinformation and adding in his hatred of Mallards and Drakes.

Precondition

Marta sees error on Albert Einstein's Wikipedia page.

Post condition

A revision is sent to a moderator for review.

  1. Marta clicks on a link in an email
  2. The link takes her to the Albert Einstein Wikipedia page.
  3. Wikipedia automatically signs Marta into her account.
  4. Marta clicks on "Article History"
  5. Marta reviews the article history to see what has been changed.
  6. Marta, after confirming her suspicions, begins a revision.
  7. Marta deletes the incorrect info and adds new info into her revision.
  8. Marta clicks "Submit"
  9. Marta's revision is sent to an administrator for approval.
  10. Marta pours a glass of Merlot, congratulating herself on a job well done.
